Introduction to Microtransat and our Boat

Microtransat

Microtransat is a competition where teams make small unmanned vessels to cross the Atlantic ocean. The general outline of the rules is that your boat must

  • Be less than 2.4 meters front to back
  • Transmit it's location at least every 6 hours
  • Be energetically autonomous
The full rules can be found on their website.

The Boat
Instead of the tradiational sailboat design, Autonautilus uses an array of solar panels to generate power to drive electric motors for propulsion. A trimaran is used to maximize area to put solar panels as well as improve stability.

A concept render demonstrating trimaran design with solar panels on top.

Key Technologies


Solar Powered Drivetrain

A large array of solar panels and batteries the boat can maintain thrust continually and power the onboard computer and navigation. This allows for long distance travel at a useful speed without the need for fossile fuels.


Galvanic Cells

High performance galvantic cells on the hull of the boat help prevent corrosion on any exposed metal of the boat. Additionally it can generate a small amount of energy to run the computer if the main solar battery runs out.

Image Source

Autonomous Navigation

Using average weather conditions such as wind, ocean currents, and sunlight the boat can plot and follow the fastest path. Additionally using publically avaliable forcast data it can change it's course to optimize for predicted weather conditions.
